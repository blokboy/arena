# Rollover is a persistent, member-only, stake-weighted vote — no separate "control" concept

ADR-0002 gave whichever backer held >50% of a leg's stake unilateral control over it, with a headcount-majority vote as the fallback when no one held a majority. Grilling surfaced two problems: (1) nothing in that design restricted control to the parlay's formal members — a non-member with enough capital could out-stake the group and seize a decision the roster was supposed to govern, undermining the entire point of a closed/invite-only roster beyond "who can append legs"; (2) the majority-or-else-vote structure needed a tiebreaker for the no-majority case, which briefly became "default to the leg's original opener" — an awkward special rule that turned out to be unnecessary.

**Decision:** rollover authority on a regular-parlay leg is a single, unified mechanism:

- A **persistent, toggleable vote**, open only to the leg's **formal-member backers** (open/non-member stakers participate economically — they share proportionally in the leg's outcome — but have no vote here at all, not even a zero-weight one).
- Each member's vote is **weighted by their current stake on that leg** (recomputed live from their active `LegStake` amount, not snapshotted at vote-cast time — a member's weight changes if they add more stake later).
- The moment the combined stake of members currently voting "yes" exceeds **50% of the leg's total member stake** (non-member stake excluded from this total), the rollover executes immediately for the **whole leg** — every backer's stake moves together, including non-members' money, even though they had no vote in the decision.
- A member whose own stake alone exceeds 50% of the member total can pass the vote by themselves — this isn't a separate "control" mechanic, it's just the case where one vote alone clears the threshold.
- If no combination of "yes" votes ever crosses 50%, **nothing happens** — the leg simply rides to its actual resolution, identical to no one having voted at all. This is a complete, well-defined default; no tiebreaker or fallback-to-opener is needed, because "the vote hasn't passed" is never an invalid state.

**Day's Parlay is unaffected** — it keeps its own separate, unchanged vote: headcount-based (one vote per backer, no stake-weighting), open to every backer (there's no member tier there at all). The two mechanisms now differ in two dimensions at once (who can vote, and how votes are weighted), both for the same underlying reason: regular parlays are small, self-selected groups where stake-weighted influence among *trusted co-creators* is acceptable; Day's Parlay is an anyone-can-back system-wide format where neither capital nor any other axis should let one participant dominate.

**Consequences:** removes the `legControl` computed field and the separate `POST .../rollover` "execute unilaterally" endpoint from Backend's plan entirely — everything routes through the vote endpoint (`POST .../rollover-vote`), which tallies and executes synchronously on every cast. Frontend's `RolloverControl` no longer branches between a "controller banner" mode and a "group vote" mode for regular parlays; it always renders the same weighted-vote UI (a live stake-weighted tally, not a headcount tally), parameterized against Day's Parlay's separate headcount-vote component only by *how* votes are weighted and *who* may cast them.
